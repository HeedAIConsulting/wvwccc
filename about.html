Our mission

Championing local business and community

We connect members to customers, to each other, and to the community — through a trusted directory, year-round events, advocacy, and programs that keep the Valley thriving.

From monthly networking breakfasts and ribbon cuttings to our signature Gala, the Food & Wine event, and Grateful Hearts first-responder fundraisers, the Chamber turns connection into opportunity. Today, with our new digital platform — a live member directory, community guides, a jobs board, real-estate listings, and the "Ask Wendy" AI concierge — that connection works around the clock.

Communities we serve

One Valley, six neighborhoods

Each community brings something different to the West Valley economy — together they're one of the strongest business corridors in Los Angeles.

The business hub

Woodland Hills

Home to the Warner Center business district, Westfield Topanga & The Village, Kaiser Permanente, and Pierce College. The Ventura Boulevard corridor hosts thousands of shops, restaurants, and professional firms.

Warner Center districtWestfield TopangaPierce College
The boomtown

Warner Center

The Valley's downtown in the making. The Warner Center 2035 Plan has already delivered 17,500+ new homes, and the $10B Rams Village will bring the LA Rams' permanent headquarters, a retail village, and two entertainment venues.

Rams Village · $10B26,000 homes planned48,000 new jobs
The medical mile

Tarzana

Named for Edgar Rice Burroughs' Tarzana Ranch, today it anchors the Valley's healthcare economy — led by Providence Cedars-Sinai Tarzana Medical Center's $542M expansion — alongside a thriving Ventura Boulevard dining and retail scene.

$542M hospital expansionVentura Blvd dining
The comeback story

Reseda

Founded in 1912 and part of the Chamber since 2014, Reseda is in the middle of a city-backed revitalization — new housing, a re-emerging Sherman Way arts and theater district, and some of the Valley's most accessible commercial space.

Est. 1912Sherman Way revival
The maker's roots

Canoga Park

The aerospace engines that took America to the Moon were built here. Today its historic downtown, antique row, and small-business corridors sit minutes from Warner Center's growth — prime ground for the next generation of makers.

Aerospace heritageHistoric downtown
The neighborhood

West Hills

A premier residential community with top-rated schools, West Hills Hospital, and neighborhood shopping centers — the customer base every West Valley business wants next door.

West Hills HospitalTop-rated schools
Our story

Nearly a century of showing up

1930

Founded as the Woodland Hills Chamber of Commerce — local merchants organizing through the Great Depression.

2014

Renamed the West Valley · Warner Center Chamber of Commerce, welcoming Reseda and reflecting a Valley-wide mission.

2024

The LA Rams arrive in Woodland Hills — the Chamber's backyard becomes home to one of the biggest developments in LA history.

2026

The Chamber launches its new digital platform — the next 100 years of connecting the West Valley, online at the same trusted home: woodlandhillscc.net.
