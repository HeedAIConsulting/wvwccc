The West Valley & Warner Center

Serving Tarzana, Woodland Hills, Reseda, and the Warner Center since 1930 — a vibrant business community at the heart of the San Fernando Valley.

A modern, business-friendly corner of Los Angeles

The West San Fernando Valley is home to a thriving mix of small businesses, modern Class-A office towers, restaurants, retail, healthcare, and entertainment — all within easy reach of the rest of the city. Whether you're opening a storefront, growing a practice, or relocating a headquarters, the West Valley and Warner Center offer connectivity, talent, and quality of life.

Green spaces, tree-lined streets, and a strong sense of neighborhood make this one of the most livable parts of LA — and a place where local businesses genuinely support one another.

The communities we serve

Six neighborhoods, 249,000+ residents, one continuous market from the Santa Monica Mountains foothills to the LA River:

Woodland Hills

70,666 residents · median age 41

The commercial heart of the West Valley — Warner Center, Westfield Topanga, Kaiser Permanente, Pierce College, and the famous Candy Cane Lane.

Warner Center

1,100 plan acres · ~20,000 homes by 2035

The Valley's downtown in the making — home of the future $10B Rams Village and the region's biggest concentration of new housing.

Tarzana

39,619 residents · median age 41

Named for Edgar Rice Burroughs' Tarzana Ranch; today the Valley's premier medical corridor, anchored by Providence Cedars-Sinai's $542M expansion.

Reseda

65,543 residents · founded 1912

A family community in the middle of Reseda Rising — the restored Reseda Theater, a $26M LA Kings ice & roller rink, and a re-emerging Sherman Way.

Canoga Park

41,261 residents · median age 36

Built the rocket engines that reached the Moon. Today: the historic Madrid Theatre, antique row, and one of LA's most diverse small-business communities.

West Hills

32,267 residents · 16 schools

The premier family-residential community — West Hills Hospital, Chaminade Prep, Orcutt Ranch, and trail access to the Simi Hills.
